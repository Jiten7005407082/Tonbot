Understanding Naming Words in English Language

What is a Noun?

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. Nouns are one of the most fundamental parts of speech and serve as the subject or object in a sentence.

1 Common Noun

A common noun is a general name for a person, place, or thing. It is not capitalized unless it begins a sentence.
Examples: teacher, city, book, river

2 Proper Noun

A proper noun is the specific name of a particular person, place, or thing. It always begins with a capital letter.
Examples: John, London, The Bible, Amazon River

3 Concrete Noun

A concrete noun names something that can be perceived through the five senses (sight, hearing, smell, taste, touch).
Examples: table, music, perfume, chocolate, silk

4 Abstract Noun

An abstract noun names an idea, quality, or state that cannot be perceived through the five senses.
Examples: love, happiness, freedom, wisdom, time

5 Countable Noun

A countable noun refers to things that can be counted. They have both singular and plural forms.
Examples: book/books, child/children, tree/trees

6 Uncountable Noun

An uncountable noun refers to things that cannot be counted. They usually do not have a plural form.
Examples: water, rice, information, advice

7 Collective Noun

A collective noun names a group of people, animals, or things considered as one unit.
Examples: team, family, flock, class

8 Compound Noun

A compound noun is made up of two or more words that function as a single noun.
Examples: toothpaste, swimming pool, mother-in-law

Noun Functions in Sentences

Nouns can serve different functions in a sentence. Understanding these roles helps in constructing grammatically correct sentences.

ꯋꯥꯍꯩꯄꯔꯦꯡꯗꯥ ꯅꯥꯎꯟꯒꯤ ꯊꯕꯛꯁꯤꯡ

ꯅꯥꯎꯟꯁꯤꯡꯅꯥ ꯋꯥꯍꯩꯄꯔꯦꯡꯗꯥ ꯃꯔꯤ ꯂꯩꯅꯕꯥ ꯊꯕꯛ ꯀꯌꯥꯅꯥ ꯇꯧꯏ꯫

Function Description Example
Subject The noun that performs the action of the verb Teachers educate students.
Object The noun that receives the action of the verb Students respect their teachers.
Indirect Object The noun that indicates to/for whom the action is done She gave her friend a gift.
Object of Preposition The noun that follows a preposition The book is on the table.
Predicate Noun The noun that follows a linking verb and renames the subject My father is a doctor.
