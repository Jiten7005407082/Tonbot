Understanding Action Words in English Grammar

What is a Verb?

A verb is a word that shows an action, occurrence, or state of being. It tells us what the subject of a sentence does or is.

1 Action Verb

An action verb shows what someone or something does.
Examples: run, eat, dance, write

ꯍꯧꯕꯥ ꯚꯔꯕ

2 Linking Verb

A linking verb connects the subject with information about it (state of being).
Examples: am, is, are, was, were

ꯋꯥꯍꯩ ꯃꯥꯍꯅꯤ ꯂꯥꯡꯕꯥ ꯚꯔꯕ

ꯈꯨꯗꯝ: ꯑꯃ, ꯏꯁ, ꯑꯥꯔ

3 Auxiliary (Helping) Verb

Helping verbs are used with main verbs to form verb phrases and indicate tenses or moods.
Examples: have, has, had, will, can

ꯄꯥꯎꯗꯣꯡꯒꯤ ꯚꯔꯕ

ꯈꯨꯗꯝ: ꯍꯥꯚ, ꯀꯦꯟ, ꯋꯤꯜ

4 Transitive Verb

A transitive verb takes a direct object that receives the action.
Examples: read a book, kick the ball

ꯇ꯭ꯔꯦꯟꯁꯤꯇ ꯚꯔꯕ

ꯈꯨꯗꯝ: ꯀꯤꯛ ꯑꯃ ꯅꯨꯡꯃꯥꯔꯤꯛ

5 Intransitive Verb

An intransitive verb does not take a direct object.
Examples: sleep, go, arrive, sit

ꯏꯟꯇ꯭ꯔꯦꯟꯁꯤꯇ ꯚꯔꯕ

ꯈꯨꯗꯝ: ꯊꯥꯛ, ꯃꯤꯠ, ꯑꯍꯣꯡ

6 Modal Verb

A modal verb expresses ability, permission, necessity, or possibility.
Examples: can, may, must, should

Verb Functions in Sentences

Verbs tell us what the subject is doing, when it happens, and the state of being in a sentence.
