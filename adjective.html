Adjective

Describing Words in English Grammar

What is an Adjective?

An adjective is a word that describes or modifies a noun or pronoun. It gives more information about the quality, quantity, size, color, shape, or other characteristics of the noun.

1 Descriptive Adjective

Describes the quality or kind of a person or thing.
Examples: beautiful girl, tall tree, red flower

2 Quantitative Adjective

Shows the quantity of a thing or how much/many.
Examples: some water, few books, many students

3 Demonstrative Adjective

Points out which person or thing is meant.
Examples: this pen, that house, those cars

4 Possessive Adjective

Shows possession or ownership.
Examples: my book, their school, his car

5 Interrogative Adjective

Used with nouns to ask questions.
Examples: which book, what color, whose bag

6 Proper Adjective

Formed from proper nouns and usually capitalized.
Examples: Indian culture, Shakespearean play

7 Numeral Adjective

Indicates number, order, or position.
Examples: three dogs, first prize, second chance

Adjectives in Sentences

Adjectives usually come before a noun or after a linking verb to describe the noun or pronoun in the sentence.

Position Description Example
Before Noun Placed directly before the noun it describes She has a beautiful dress.
After Linking Verb Placed after verbs like ‘is’, ‘seems’, or ‘looks’ The sky is blue.
Comparative Form Used to compare two nouns or pronouns This car is faster than that one.
Superlative Form Used to show the highest degree of quality He is the tallest boy in the class.
