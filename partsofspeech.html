What is a Sentence?

📝

A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought. It typically contains a subject and a predicate.

Example of a sentence: Rita plays football. → This expresses a complete thought.

Example of a non-sentence: Rita nice very football. → This doesn't make meaningful sense.

5 Types of Sentences

1. Declarative/ Ashertive Sentence (Statement)

Makes a statement or expresses an opinion.

ꯋꯥꯐꯝ ꯅꯇ_ꯔꯥꯒ ꯃꯣꯠ ꯑꯃꯥ ꯐꯣꯡꯗꯣꯛꯞ ꯋꯥꯍꯩ ꯄꯔꯦꯡꯕꯨ ꯑꯦꯁꯦꯔꯇꯤꯞ ꯀꯧꯏ:

English: Rita sings a song.

Meitei Mayek: ꯔꯤꯇꯥꯟ ꯏꯁꯩ ꯑꯃꯥ ꯁꯛꯏ꯫

2. Interrogative Sentence (Question)

Asks a question. Ends with a question mark.

ꯋꯥꯍꯪ ꯑꯃꯥ ꯍꯪꯕ ꯋꯥꯍꯩ ꯄꯔꯦꯡꯕꯨ ꯏꯟꯇ_ꯔꯣꯒꯦꯇꯤꯞ ꯁꯦꯟꯇꯦꯟꯁ ꯀꯧꯏ꯫

English: Does Rita play football?

Meitei Mayek: ꯔꯤꯇꯥꯅꯥ ꯐꯨꯠꯕꯣꯜ ꯁꯥꯅꯕ_ꯔꯥ?

3. Imperative Sentence (Command)

Gives a command, makes a request, or gives instructions. Ends with a period or exclamation mark.

ꯍꯥꯏꯖꯕꯥ ꯊꯧꯗꯥꯕ ꯑꯃꯗꯤ ꯌꯥꯊꯪꯄꯤꯕ ꯋꯥꯍꯩꯄꯔꯦꯡꯕꯨ ꯏꯃꯄꯔꯦꯇꯤꯞ ꯁꯦꯟꯇꯦꯟꯁ ꯀꯧꯏ꯫

English: Please pass the ball.

Meitei Mayek: ꯆꯥꯟꯕꯤꯗꯨꯟ ꯕꯣꯜ ꯑꯗꯨ ꯄꯤꯕꯤꯔꯛꯎ꯫

4. Exclamatory Sentence (Exclamation)

Expresses strong emotion or surprise. Ends with an exclamation mark.

ꯑꯉꯛꯄꯥ ꯅꯇ_ꯔꯥꯒ ꯑꯀꯟꯕ ꯏꯍꯨꯜ ꯐꯣꯡꯗꯣꯀꯄꯥ ꯋꯥꯍꯩ ꯄꯔꯦꯡꯕꯨ ꯑꯦꯁꯀ_ꯂꯥꯃꯦꯇꯣꯔꯤ ꯁꯦꯟꯇꯦꯟꯁ ꯀꯧꯏ꯫

English: ya My mother is coming !

Meitei Mayek: ꯅꯨꯉꯥꯏꯔꯦ ꯏꯃꯥ ꯂꯥꯛꯂꯦ!

5. Optative Sentence (Wish or Prayer)

Expresses a wish, prayer, or hope.

ꯊꯧꯅꯤꯖꯕ ꯅꯇ_ꯔꯥꯒ ꯅꯤꯖꯕꯥ ꯋꯥꯍꯩ ꯄꯔꯦꯡꯕꯨ ꯑꯣꯞꯇꯦꯞꯇꯤꯞ ꯁꯦꯟꯇꯦꯟꯁ ꯀꯧꯏ꯫

English: May you succeed in your efforts.

Meitei Mayek: ꯅꯡꯅꯥ ꯍꯣꯠꯅꯖꯕꯥ ꯄꯨꯝꯕꯥ ꯃꯥꯢꯄꯥꯛꯄꯥ ꯑꯣꯏꯌꯨ꯫
