What are Parts of Speech?

Parts of speech are the categories to which words are assigned based on their syntactic functions and semantic roles in a sentence. English has eight primary parts of speech that help us understand how words function in different contexts.

1 Noun

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.
Examples: teacher, school, book, happiness

2 Pronoun

A pronoun is a word that takes the place of a noun to avoid repetition.
Examples: he, she, it, they, we, you

3 Verb

A verb expresses an action, occurrence, or state of being.
Examples: run, think, is, have, become

4 Adjective

An adjective describes or modifies a noun or pronoun.
Examples: beautiful, tall, smart, five

5 Adverb

An adverb describes or modifies a verb,or another adverb.
Examples: quickly, very, well, yesterday

6 Preposition

A preposition shows the relationship between a noun/pronoun and other words in a sentence.
Examples: in, on, at, with, between

7 Conjunction

A conjunction connects words, sentences, or clauses.
Examples: and, but, or, because, although

8 Interjection

An interjection expresses strong emotion or surprise and stands alone.
Examples: Wow! Ouch! Hey! Oh no!
